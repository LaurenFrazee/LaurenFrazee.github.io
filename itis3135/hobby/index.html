Hobby: Being A Struggling Artist

About Me

I'm a DIY lover who enjoys creating things from scratch. I like to paint, build, and craft anything that I think I can make. I started doing DIY during the pandemic when Home Depot was my only escape. DIY has also been a great way to save money, well, maybe not if you count the tools that I have or will buy. On this website, I show you my DIY projects, suggestions, and instructions on how to DIY your way to...debt..creativy. I hope you find some inspiration and join me in this creative journey!

When do I DIY

My DIY hobby is something that I enjoy doing whenever I have some free time. Depending on the project, it can take me anywhere from a few hours to a few days to complete it. I like to plan ahead, gather the materials, and follow the instructions carefully. Sometimes I encounter challenges or make mistakes, but I always learn from them and improve my skills. I feel proud and satisfied when I finish a DIY project and see the result of my hard work.


The best time to do my hobbies is during the summer, when school is out and I have more time and energy. I prefer to do my DIY projects in the early morning, when it is not too hot and the sun is bright. I set up my workspace in the backyard, where I can enjoy the fresh air and the nature. I find it relaxing and fun to work on my hobbies in the morning, and t hen I have the rest of the day to do other things or relax.

Where

Being a struggling Artistic/chronic DIYer is messy, however, I love to create things with my own hands. I started doing it in the garage, but I soon realized that it was not the best place for it. The sawdust was everywhere, which may me feel anxious to clean up. I decided to move to the backyard, where I had more space and fresh air.


However, I did not know that the sawdust could have a negative impact on the grass. I learned, the hard way, that it can deplete the nitrogen in the soil.There was a semicircle of dead grass where I was using my table saw. I am still trying to fix that problem; that's an on going project for another day. Now, i work patio, where I can work without harming the grass. I Should invest in some sort of dust catcher $$.

How

Whenever I’m in a creative mood, it’s often sparked by scrolling through Pinterest. I’ll stumble upon an item that I can’t locate in any store, and then I’ll set out to figure out how to create it myself. At other times, I turn to YouTube for instructional videos. My first significant DIY project was a 2x4 sectional, which I completed by following plans from Ana-White.com. It didn’t turn out perfectly, but it’s still standing, a testament to my effort.


Overall, i was shocked at how i just needed some basic tool, and a miter saw to complete it. YUP! It all started with a miter saw!

Why

DIYing combines three of my favorite things: creativity, problem-solving, and cost-saving. I find it incredibly satisfying when I have all my tools, supplies, and a great playlist at hand. I can lose myself for hours in a project, oblivious to the world around me. Of course, not everything always goes according to plan. I’ve had my fair share of failed projects, but each one has taught me something new. Now, I jokingly say that I know a hundred ways not to cut wood. My pragmatic approach to everything is to remind myself that in 2023, there’s almost always a blueprint available for whatever I want to create. It’s all about learning, adapting, and enjoying the journey. But seriously, I love home decor just as much as i love saving; this is the middle ground;
